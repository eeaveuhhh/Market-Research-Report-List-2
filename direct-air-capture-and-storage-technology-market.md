<p><p>Direct Air Capture and Storage Technology is a method of removing carbon dioxide directly from the air and then storing it in a suitable storage medium. There are two main types of markets for this technology: Physical Absorption in Liquid Media and Adsorption on Solid Media. In Physical Absorption, carbon dioxide is absorbed into a liquid solvent like amines, while in Adsorption, it is trapped on a solid material like activated carbon. Both methods offer efficient ways to capture and store carbon dioxide emissions from the atmosphere.</p></p>
