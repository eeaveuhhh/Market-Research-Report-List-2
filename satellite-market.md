<p><p>Satellites can be classified into different market types based on their orbits. Low Earth Orbit (LEO) satellites operate at an altitude of up to 2,000 kilometers, providing fast communication with low latency. Geostationary Earth Orbit (GEO) satellites orbit at a fixed point above the Earth's equator at an altitude of about 36,000 kilometers, offering wide coverage for communication services. Medium Earth Orbit (MEO) satellites are positioned at an altitude of around 10,000-20,000 kilometers, supporting navigation and communication applications. Other market types include Highly Elliptical Orbit (HEO) and Polar Orbit satellites for specific purposes.</p></p>
