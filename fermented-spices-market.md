<p><ul><li>Acetic Acid Bacteria</li><li>Lactic Acid Bacteria</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Fermented spices market can be categorized into two types based on the predominant bacteria involved in the fermentation process - Acetic Acid Bacteria and Lactic Acid Bacteria. Acetic Acid Bacteria produce acetic acid during fermentation, giving a tangy flavor to the spices. Lactic Acid Bacteria, on the other hand, produce lactic acid which imparts a mild, sour taste. Both types of bacteria play a crucial role in enhancing the flavor, aroma, and shelf-life of fermented spices, making them popular choices among consumers worldwide.</p></p>
