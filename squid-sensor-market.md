<p><strong>The SQUID Sensor Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Low-Temperature SQUID</li><li>High-Temperature SQUID</li></ul></p>
<p><p>SQUID sensors can be categorized into two types based on the operating temperature: Low-Temperature SQUID and High-Temperature SQUID. Low-Temperature SQUID sensors operate at cryogenic temperatures, typically below 4 Kelvin, to achieve high sensitivity and resolution for applications such as biomagnetic measurements. On the other hand, High-Temperature SQUID sensors operate at higher temperatures, typically above 77 Kelvin, making them easier to use and maintain compared to low-temperature SQUIDs. Both types of SQUID sensors have their own advantages and are used in various scientific and medical fields.</p></p>
