<p><p>Giant Magnetoresistive Heads (GMR) are used in hard disk drives to read and write data. There are two main types of GMR heads: Film Type and Needle Type. Film Type GMR heads use multiple thin film layers to detect changes in the magnetic field, allowing for higher storage densities. Needle Type GMR heads have a more traditional design with a needle-like probe, offering better sensitivity and resolution for reading and writing data. Both types play a vital role in the development and advancement of hard drive technology.</p></p>
