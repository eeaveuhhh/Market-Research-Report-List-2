<p><p>DC Distribution Networks can be divided into three main market types based on voltage levels: Low Voltage, Medium Voltage, and High Voltage. Low Voltage networks typically operate at voltages below 1 kV and are commonly used for residential and small commercial applications. Medium Voltage networks operate at voltages between 1 kV and 69 kV, serving larger commercial and industrial customers. High Voltage networks operate at voltages above 69 kV and are used primarily for transmission and substation applications, connecting different parts of the grid.</p></p>
